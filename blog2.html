Introduction to Industry 4.0 and Society 5.0

Industry 4.0 has been one of the newest phases with advanced strategical approaches in the industrial revolution. The concepts under the umbrella of Industry 4.0 mainly lay weight on interaction, intercommunication, Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML), and so on. However, Industry 4.0 is not just a breakthrough where a new technology was being implemented to a great extend with regards to bringing about improvements and enhancements in the manufacturing field. The main aim of Industry 4.0 is to emerge with ground breaking and revolutionary ideas and strategies which would, in turn, take the holistic business approach forward. In fact, it’s often considered as a very organized and structured revolution to bring about these aesthetic changes in the functioning of the industrial platforms. For majority of the problems and issues faced by the industrial facilitation, Industry 4.0 has always tried to bring in an autonomous and optimized solutional implementation. Some of the main parameters which are touched upon by the developers of the solution during this revolution include: curating solution sector wise, providing customer centric experiences, and the option of reducing the generated volume of manufactured products, taking flexibility to the next level, and leading a sustainable platform.

One of the other such revolutionary concepts is Society 5.0 which takes its grass roots from the Japanese government. Its main focus was to form a societal concept which is a strong bridged community of cyber and physical space so as to strike a balance in the economic development using the required goods and latent. In simpler words, this societal advancement focuses on revamping the existential relations between the society and technology and that of relations which endow technologically facilitated relations between the community. Society 5.0 has a hierarchical path which leads down to Society 1.0 (hunting society), Society 2.0 (agricultural society), Society 3.0 (industrial society), Society 4.0 (information society) and Society 5.0 (smart society). The proposal for this concept was initiated in the fifth Science and Technology Basic Plan as an idea for the futuristic society that Japan should aspire to become in the coming years. By closely observing the intricate issues and problems faced by the community in the current society, the reforms for Society 5.0 were carefully laid out. Achieving a society with a forward mind set and futuristic thinking which highlights mutual respect for the individuals of the society and the provide freedom and an enthusiastic life. Scope of IoT in Industry 4.0 and Society 5.0

From a plethora of aspects these concepts have evolved holistically leading to healthy, innovative and smart trends being propagated. When it comes to Industry 4.0, one of the highly observable trends is the utilization of intelligent entities to inculcate features of adaptability, efficiency, and integrity. In fact, majority of the applications which are considered to be ‘Smart’ are an extension of the trends set in by Industry 4.0, including those of smart manufacturing, smart products, smart cities, and so on.

Applications like smart factories and industries work with extensive interfaces and platforms which facilitate command execution, transfer of data, and interaction and cohesiveness among the individual nodes and devices in the system. Industry 4.0 manages to scale up the applications (all applications from agriculture to weather) to a top-notch level by contributing with respect to the number of nascent and novel ideas, strategies, and techniques that can enhance the optimal results obtained by the systems like Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT) cloud-based approaches, etc. One of the other major trends which has been impacted by Industry 4.0 is a wave of competition to all other companies in the industry as they struggle to overcome the curbs and challenges of their business with respect to cost, worthiness, and what not. Industry 4.0 versus Society 5.0

Though there are quite a few similarities in the aims, goals, and visions with regards to Industry 4.0 and Society 5.0, the differences make each of these concepts uniquely distinct and draw a fine line between them. Industry 4.0 is more often considered as a network and graph which interlinks the various applicable machine systems, automated devices, and entities in the field of industrial management. This is to ensure a stronger sense of interaction and communication among the participants in the development phase of any industrial commitment. The three major paradigms which govern the existence of this industrial revolution are as follows:

  • Intelligence of products: This assures that the operational and logistical data related to the products are stored within them and follows a standardized procedure so that they can coordinate and interact between the different resources required during production.
  • Intelligence of machines: This is an essential component and paradigm as the machines and devices used for the infrastructural development of the industries are to be made enhanced so as to replace the traditional production flow with a more decentralized and flexible production line.
  • Integration of CPS: CPS is one of the other evolving factors which contributes significantly to the growth and improvement of the implementational factor of Industry 4.0.

A lot of improvisational elements in the industrial sector have been powered by the concept of Industry 4.0. One such example is that of smart factories wherein efficiency of resources, acclimatization, and smartness of entities are focused on to get enhanced production results. Further, the concept of this industrial revolution spreads a healthy competitive vibe among all other competitors in the market which constantly pushes the business makers to perform at their best.

On the other hand, Society 5.0 is more about centering humans in the evolutionary process for heading towards a healthier and prosperous life for benefitting the human society. It is often said that Industry 4.0 is considered to be the driving fuel for Society 5.0 and Japan has already begun its exploration amidst this revolution. Parallel intelligence is the key concept, or rather, the heart and soul of Society 5.0. Parallel intelligence is considered as an extension to artificial intelligence except that it is highly integrated and linked to cyber physical systems. The ultimate aim and goal of Society 5.0 is not only to just mechanize and automate each and every domain but also ensure sustainability in the lifestyle and development by achieving a super smart society.

History of previous industrial and society revolutions

It started with the very first industrial revolution Industry 1.0 in the late 18th century. This revolution is what sparked up the urge for introducing the world to mechanical production techniques to perpetuate the mass production of goods with ease. It mainly focused on the quality, efficiency, and scale of the techniques and strategies involved and significant changes could be observed as small factories and stores were evolving into large organizations, serving a large number of audiences. The next revolutionary step in the industrial revolution was Industry 2.0 which commenced in the beginning of the twentieth century. When the previous revolution focused on bringing in mechanical concepts to production, Industry 2.0 specifically aimed towards bringing in electric power to facilitate the machines and devices. These devices and systems were indeed a turning point for the industrialists as it was much easier and less cumbersome to operate on these machines with enhanced results and optimum efficiency. Another significant change that came about during this era was the introduction to efficient management systems and frameworks. A variety of production management strategies were adopted which ultimately led to enhanced quality results. Following this revolution came Industry 3.0 which commenced in the later stages of the twentieth century where there was a surge in the inventions and innovations brought about with respect to electronic devices using transistors and integrated circuits.

One of the significant achievements in this revolutionary stage was to automate the strategies and techniques being used leading to massive speed, more accuracy, and reducing human interference to the maximum. A notable observation was also faced with regards to the software and not just the hardware applications. Software applications were now capable of not just controlling the hardware components but were put to use for resource planning, inventory management, and so on. In other words, this was also the revolutionary point where the tech industry/IT field took birth. Finally, the fourth industrial revolution is what brought about massive changes across the globe, connecting people from all over the world through networking, telecommunication, and a plethora of other advanced technologies. The incorporation of CPS has further aggravated the strategy to communicate efficiently with various devices and nodes efficiently and robustly.

Many research and analogies have reciprocated that the very first model of society, Society 1.0 was the hunting community. This society mainly aimed towards coexisting with nature so as to lead a life by taking the benefits reaped from hunting. Following this model of society came Society 2.0 which was the Agrarian society. This society commenced towards the end of the 18th century and focused on achieving various agricultural improvements while reaping benefits that they acquired by sowing seeds. This form of a society has been a rock-solid framework which is followed to a great extent in many countries across the world for a living. Then, came Society 3.0 which intersected with the industrial revolution wherein more focus was given to locomotives and mass production. This form of society was also one of the rooted societies which has been a turning point as it focused on easing out the tedious production strategies. Society 4.0 resonates with the information society wherein the goal was to ultimately achieve the digital transformation and add a new dimension to the common lifestyle. Various computers and gadgets were introduced which could keep the society well connected and informed of the happening around the world and since then, there hasn’t been a turn back. Society 5.0 is an extended version of the above-mentioned societies which aims to improvise on each of the mentioned fields leading to a sustainable and smart society.
