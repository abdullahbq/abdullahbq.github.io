CHAPTER 4

Role of AI in Smart Cyber Security for IoT-based Cloud Applications

Introduction

Today, every device (communication) is getting connected to the Internet or with a public network to do everyday/daily tasks. These devices/smart devices (Internet of Things devices) are creating a lot of data that has features like veracity, volume, value, and so on. Many applications/sectors (using these devices) like consumer products, retail, banking, insurance, automotive, and so on, are getting several cyber-attacks or vulnerabilities. We need to detect these attacks in the minimum time to avoid maximum loss. Note that if this attack occurs in a critical business or sector like healthcare, it may be cost us losing someone’s life. Since decades, cyber security is a primary concern for online users/active users on the web. But today’s cyber security is the need of the hour because it is the need of every industry/business. We are looking forward to provide innovative solutions to avoid such attacks or vulnerabilities, mitigated on a public network/infrastructure using artificial intelligence. IoTs produce huge amount of data which is stores in cloud and can be accessed anywhere, anytime with valid/authorised users. To secure this data, this article discusses the uses of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in intrusion detection/vulnerability detection, and so on, and reduces the workload of cyber-security professionals. Also, various other points like benefits, pitfalls, future uses, and other possible uses of AI in some other sectors also have been discussed in this chapter.

Introduction to Artificial Intelligence

The world is shifting to the digital-era with a fast rate, i.e., everything/everyone is moving towards digitalization like various entertainment and business sources, recent trends, announcement of new products, and so on, and this change will change even more than this current growth rate. In digitalization, users/consumers get satisfied with an instant response from the service provider. There are many useful conveniences and benefits of digital era, including several negatives. For example, one popular negative concern/destructive threat is that it keeps/poses our private (personal) information at risk now. In the previous decade, we have encountered/mitigated hundreds of cases related to theft of identity, data leakage, and money loss. In general, cyberattacks are inescapable and do affect individuals, corporations, and government bodies. Currently, the world has virtually reached a stage where cybercriminals can easily carry out their targets using smart things (including their intelligence) properly. Hence, today the need for cybersecurity has never been more critical and dangerous than before.

AI is an extraordinary component of information technology that involves the emergence of a computer that behaves and operates like a human mind. The main aspects of AI include the analogy of human senses. The machine is capable of understanding touch and voice as attributes that are put within the system without the aid of humans to operate the possible tasks of the regular life situation. AI, however, is the analysis of intelligence agents that involves the state of the world and successfully attains its target. The plurality of structures in the computer environment is designed to fulfil the purposes according to the essence of the case with the application of the special characteristics from the natural current features of the person. Problems/risks in the current scenario

As discussed earlier, IoT0073 are currently in use in many applications/sectors to reduce the workload and increase the efficiency of many businesses/industries. But as the first risk, these smart devices are also facing several open challenges/issues. We need to protect IoT and Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT) sensors and systems against any cyber-attacks/tempering. Adversarial AI has been introduced as risk to human based-based cyber security; it refers to the development and use of AI for malicious purposes/spreading malicious. Cyber attackers or criminals can use AI to improve their attempts/cyber-attacks (working as an enemy). Adversarial AI means a user/hacker can use AI for his/her use in performing an attack. Also, monitoring each ‘packet’ of data, i.e., each part of networks communications is almost impossible for human analysts to monitor or track/verify accurately. Human experts might take several days to weeks to fully understand these policies and make sure the security implementation is successful.

We need to recognize the possible risks associated with adversarial AI or reduce the workload of human experts. Some examples of adversarial AI are as follows:

  • Chatbots, etc.
  • Consider cases wherein a cybercriminal produces an adversarial image which overcomes facial recognition software. For example, an iPhone X’s ‘FaceID’ access characteristics makes use of neural networks to identify and spot faces, which makes it prone to contradictory AI attacks.

AI has the ability to respond very quickly to cyber-attacks which would take significant amount of time (based on the scenario) to recognize humans. With such speed, we need to avoid such unauthorized access (attempts) in digitally-based business models, and require a password-less world, which will be more dependable on AI (machines). AI should develop an efficient approach which raises privacy alarms in case of leaking any user’s/customer’s privacy. Note that more dependability on AI may also a cause for revealing the user’s privacy to other machines or users.

Today’s cyber security requires more basic needs than before. Preserving the privacy/identity is a primary concern in the twenty-first century; also the new security perimeter which requires a Zero Trust Security framework to be secure against serious criminal/cyber threats/attacks. In the near future, we can detect cyber threats using AI and predict threats on a network, that is, threat hunting, also can respond accordingly. In other words, AI takes advantages against fraudsters and hackers. For example, PetSmart (a US-based specialty retailer), was able to save up to $12M by using AI in fraud detection from Kountet al. AI is helping under-resourced security operations analysts stay ahead of threats. We need to identify, recognize, and react to threats spontaneously, i.e., solutions to the digital security need to be profound and highly responsive. Like investment banks use predictive data models to forecast markets, insurance companies to predict accidents, or retailers to figure out the optimal time to send us a special offer; soon user authentication would rely on similar data analytics to protect users against serious threats.

Several popular tools/security tools like Maltego, OWASP Zed Attack Proxy (ZAP), Snort, Wireshark, and AI, etc. can help us in detection, prediction, and providing response. Machine Learning (ML)-based software frameworks potentially make use of numerous techniques which include statistical analysis, keyword cross-checking, ambiguity spotting, and so on to check whether a particular data packet varies from the baseline of training data sets. Areas where AI adds values in cybersecurity for detecting cyber-attacks are large-scale data analysis and anomaly detection. AI needs to build such that it should be able to monitor every event on the public network and respond to their respective user about the occurrence of an incident. Also, for a strong security or secure environment, we can use Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) wherever possible on a network.

Benefits of using Artificial Intelligence in the twenty-first century

We know that as digital businesses grow, their risk of cyber-attacks exponentially increases in the respective businesses. Detecting breach attempts today without the use of AI technologies is time consuming and a costly process. AI is used to help identify threats and thwart attacks. Note that cybersecurity inherently involves repetitiveness and tediousness. It reduces the workload of engineers, and it is the necessity of the future. The following are the listed benefits of AI:

  • To handle voluminous security data
  • Picking out threat needles in cyber haystacks
  • To increase detection and response times
  • To held the head high for AI in the arms race
  • Breathing space for human cyber security teams

Hence, AI can be trained and used more as a savior for many businesses rather than as enemy for the same.

Open discussion: Required changes in the near future with respect to cyber security

Today, one of the most vulnerable and fastest-growing threat surfaces is mobile phones. Data is at the core of cyber security trends and at risk. Threats which revolve around our workplace would result in exposing the entire establishment to vulnerabilities at frequent intervals which would ultimately lead to data breaches. We need to provide the “trust but verify” approach, low complexity, and high benefits; it reduces the time in identifying fraud or attack detection. Artificial Intelligence in cyber security is beneficial because it improves “how security experts analyse, study, and understands cybercrime.” In the coming days, identifying fraudulent activities, malware and intrusion detection, scoring risk in a network, and so on are few of the highly used AI test cases for enhancing cybersecurity. Using AI in businesses or by industries will increase the trust or bond with consumers. Consumers can rely and trust more on their business partners. It advances cyber security methods which are implemented in companies to overthrow cybercriminals and ensures the safety of the organizations and customers.

As days pass by, AI has been proving itself to be an arena of throbbing interest and extensive investments within the cyber safety society. It potentially monitors the suspicious pattern in the ordinary data classifying it as cyber-threats (like phishing, and so on.). In summary, artificial intelligence cyber security measures improve the digital security and help in digital forensics and threat hunting (also threat anticipation, incident response) process.

AI is penetrating deep in cyber security, but what about the human interface and reaction for solving the problem. Are we in a stage where computers can take human decisions? In our views, it is a better way to analyse data by ourself than to do by computers, i.e., removing the probability of revealing the user’s information or protecting systems against security breaches that can think and do in nanoseconds tasks (working as a savior). Also, we can start using blockchain-based solutions to avoid security-breaches or any attacks on a network. It needs to be pointed out that the security products which are developed on the AI framework are not less expensive and this indeed, puts forward a dilemma for majority of the small-scale businesses currently. However, a rapid response to this issue would never be to hire a large supporting team to develop custom cybersecurity systems as a solution to this problem. In summary, we require more trained people, that is, data science professionals or data engineers and cyber security professionals in the near future to fulfil and detect cyber-crimes in early stages. Future suggestions

Context-based, risk-based, and implicit authentications are the essential elements of a secured business/infrastructure. We require tighter and stronger security (using hash and digital signature mechanisms, and so on.) for our infrastructure against popular worms, viruses (like Stuxnet), and so on, attacks on respective systems/infrastructures. We have discussed several advances in artificial intelligence security tools (in the near future) and “how the technology impacts organizations, cybercriminals, and consumers alike.” Few ways where AI and ML can make a huge difference towards cybersecurity are as follows:

  • AI, password protection, and authentication.
  • AI-ML in phishing detection and prevention control.
  • Usage of Artificial Intelligence-Machine Learning (AI-ML) in vulnerability management.
  • Network Security and Artificial Intelligence.
  • Cloud-based Single-Sign-On (SSO) or dual authentication methods are gaining wider adoption by many applications by doing verification via SMS or software-based security. Today Dual authentication feature (in many applications) is on higher priority SSO is somewhere in the middle.
  • In frictionless authentication concepts like zero log-in and implicit authentication, where a system or device can use sensors and machine learning algorithms to simply recognise us by our behaviors. So, the requirement of a password-less world is the next step in computing the environment (using AI). Accessibility of things will be provided via fast biometric recognition (low friction identity management) using AI.
  • AI uses blockchain for providing secure infrastructure or attack-free infrastructure.

Note that when we integrated AI into our cyber security solutions, our cyber security analysts and other IT security employees need to know “how to effectively use it.” Cost, resources, and training are some components of artificial intelligence-based cyber security protection software. In the near future, we need an equal balance of convenience/balance among security and privacy components in an infrastructure (or business).
